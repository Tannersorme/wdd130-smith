Fruit Information

Select a fruit from the left grid.

Lime

Limes are very high in vitamin C.

Lemon

Eating lemons may actually decrease your likelihood of receiving life-threatening sickness.

Orange

One of the few existing “things” in the world whose color is also its name.

Pear

Pears actually originate from the “rose” plant family.

Apple

There are over 7,500 different types of apple “cultivars”, or variations.

Blueberry

Blueberries are one of the only foods that are naturally blue.

Strawberry

Strawberries are made up about 91% water.

Grape

Grapes have been cultivated for over 8,000 years.

Watermelon

The largest grown watermelon weighs up to 350 pounds.

Banana

Although a banana might become gross after turning brown, it is a good time to use them as banana bread if it reaches that state.

Coconut

This fruit is not considered a “true nut” but instead as a “drupe” based on its biology.

Pineapple

Despite its name this fruit does not fall in the category of a “pine” or an “apple” but is instead considered a berry.

Mango

Mangoes are sometimes referred to as “The King of Fruits”.

Peach

In some cultures, the peach is considered a symbol of good luck, happiness, & prosperity.

Cherry

Cherries contain melatonin which makes them good foods to regulate sleep patterns.

Apricot

Apricot wood is prized for being used to make musical instruments & some furniture.

Plum

Plums can aid in weight loss by being one of the foods that tricks the consumer into feeling full.

Avocado

Its very popular to mistake an avocado as a vegetable, but it is actually a berry.

Raspberry

A singular raspberry is made up of 100 tiny “druplets” that each have a seed.

Kiwi

This fruit shares the same name as a bird, but they are not related at all.
